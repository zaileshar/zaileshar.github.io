Project Overview

Design and analysis of a telescopic CMOS operational amplifier focused on high gain, low noise and low power for mixed-signal front-ends. This page summarizes architecture, design choices, simulated performance and how to reproduce results.

Key goals
  • High open-loop gain (>60 dB target)
  • Phase margin & stability for given load (PM & unity-gain)
  • Low power consumption and compact layout footprint

Design notes
  • Telescopic topology chosen for high gain and low noise at moderate supply.
  • Cascode transistors used to increase output resistance and DC gain.
  • Bias network provides stable tail current and cascode bias points.

Simulated Results & Waveform

Sample simulated metrics (illustrative)
  • Open-loop gain: ~72 dB (simulated DC)
  • GBW: ~45 MHz (unity-gain)
  • Phase margin: ~55° (with 2 pF load)
  • Power: ~0.85 mW @ 1.2 V (typical)
